Welcome to PYMS

"I am delighted to introduce our full offer to schools for the 26/27 academic year."

— Jozzy Mudzingwa, Music Service Lead

This has been an exciting year for our music service, as we have welcomed a number of new schools and continued to expand our reach, providing more children and young people with opportunities to engage in music. We recognise, however, the ongoing financial pressures faced by both schools and music services, and we remain committed to reviewing our offer to ensure it continues to provide value and affordability.

As part of this review, we have made the difficult decision to reduce the subsidy for our "Everyone Plays In Class" programme (formerly known as First Access). Schools will now be required to contribute an annual fee of £800 per academic year, with the rest subsidised by the music service. Despite this change, our pricing remains below the national average, reflecting our commitment to keeping music education accessible for all schools in Plymouth.

From September 2026, parents will be able to book individual and small group lessons directly through the music service website, significantly reducing administrative demands on schools.

Following successful trials over recent months, our music technology offer is now underway. This includes tailored DJ workshops and peripatetic teaching for individuals and small groups. As we continue to develop this area, availability will be limited — we encourage early booking to secure sessions.

PYMS continues to share and reinforce the government's commitments to ensuring every child has access to high-quality music provision. This includes opportunities to sing, play instruments, create music collaboratively, and progress further where appropriate.

In response, we will be placing increased focus on developing clear musical pathways for pupils, including ensembles, performance opportunities, and progression routes both within schools and the wider community.

We kindly encourage you to submit your instrument preferences for September as early as possible to help avoid waiting lists, and to follow our social media channels for updates.

EPIC — Everybody Plays In Class

KS2 · Formerly First Access
Whole class instrumental tuition for KS2 pupils. A PYMS tutor will plan and teach the sessions, in collaboration with the class teacher where appropriate. SEND provision is included.

Key Information

Duration
30 sessions per academic year, 60 minutes per lesson
Cost
£800 per class per year (remaining costs subsidised by the music service)
Group size
One class, up to 32 pupils
Instruments available
Brass, Clarinet, Djembe, Gamelan, Guitar, Samba, Steel Pans (Jumbie Jams), Strings, Taiko, Ukulele, and more

Benefits

  • PlanningTailored planning session between a PYMS music tutor and the class teacher
  • Curriculum integrationOngoing specialist advice including music curriculum guidance and signposting
  • Range of instrumentsOption to change instruments bi-annually, subject to availability

ISG — Individual & Small Group Tuition

KS2+
Lessons for individuals or small groups of children learning the same instrument, led by an expert PYMS tutor. SEND-experienced tutors are available to support all learners.

Key Information

Duration
30 sessions per year (recommended 30 minutes per session)
Cost
£36 per hour. 50% of fees for children receiving Free School Meals (FSM) is covered by the school.
Group size
Individual or small group (maximum 4 pupils)
Instruments available
Brass, DJ Skills, Guitar, Keyboard, Percussion, Strings, Woodwind, and more

Benefits

  • Advanced learningPupils get more contact time with highly-trained tutors
  • FlexibilitySessions can be delivered during school hours, at lunchtime, or after school
  • Small group settingA calmer, more focused environment for pupils

Music Workshops

KS2–KS5
One-off workshops tailored to fit your curriculum, offering pupils the chance to try multiple instruments. SEND-specific equipment is available. Designed to inspire and engage pupils with music, potentially for the first time.

Key Information

Duration
Half-day or full-day workshops in schools and community settings
Cost
Half-day: £175 · Full-day: £325
Group size
Up to 30 pupils, dependent on the type of workshop

Benefits

  • Curriculum integrationWorks alongside existing music provision to support delivery of government targets
  • Range of instrumentsPupils can try multiple instruments across different instrument families
  • Fun & dynamicEmphasis on learning new skills and enjoying music

Pop-Up Concerts

KS2–KS5
A surprise, informal, close-up music performance at your school — designed to be engaging, interactive, and inspiring for all pupils. These live performances can take place at any time during the school day, including break times and lunchtimes.

Key Information

Duration
Up to 45 minutes
Cost
1 FREE pop-up per year. Additional pop-ups subsidised at £45 per musician.
Location
Can take place anywhere in school. Highly interactive, focused on creating a fun and engaging atmosphere.

Benefits

  • Inspiration and aspirationSparks interest in learning an instrument and pursuing musical goals
  • Flexible timingsDeliverable at a convenient time that best suits your school
  • Engaging and excitingHighly interactive and focused on creating a fun atmosphere

Live Performance

KS2–KS5
A live musical experience for pupils and staff — more formally organised than a Pop-Up Concert, and most likely to take place during a school assembly.

Key Information

Duration
Up to 45 minutes
Cost
£100
Group size
Whole class or whole school. Best delivered during a school assembly.

Benefits

  • Professional musiciansPupils hear music delivered by highly skilled PYMS tutors
  • Engaging and excitingEach performance is highly interactive and focused on fun
  • Impact timeQuickly introduces pupils to diverse musical styles and traditions

  • Advice for SEND and inclusive practice
  • Teacher training
  • Singing and composition
  • Music observations

Benefits

Instrument Delivery & Collection

PYMS provides instruments as part of the EPIC package, delivering to and collecting from schools and academy trusts across Plymouth. Schools can also loan a class set of instruments from Devon Music Education Hub (DMEH) by completing a loan application form on the DMEH website.

Key Information

Delivery & collection
Free as part of EPIC and ISG provision
Self-collection
Free
Extra trips
£50

Benefits

  • Cost effectiveFunded instruments for EPIC and ISG provision
  • Less adminPYMS takes care of all logistics, reducing strain on your admin staff
  • Minimal storage neededInstruments can be moved at the end of term to free up storage space
